Cultural Activities

  • Sarhul Festival: A spring festival celebrated by tribal communities, marking the worship of nature and ancestors. It's a time for traditional dances, music, and prayers to the sacred Sal tree.
  • Karam Festival: A harvest festival dedicated to the Karam tree, symbolizing sibling love and nature conservation. The festival features colorful processions, folk dances, and music performances.
  • Sohrai Festival: A cattle festival celebrated during Diwali, where cattle are decorated and worshipped. It's a time to express gratitude to animals for their contribution to agriculture.
  • Tusu Parab: A harvest festival celebrated by young girls, where they create idols of Tusu with clay, straw, and colorful cloth. The festival symbolizes fertility, prosperity, and invokes blessings for a good harvest.
  • Bandna Festival: A festival dedicated to cattle, where animals are decorated, fed, and worshipped. It's a celebration of the bond between humans and animals.
  • Chhath Puja: A festival dedicated to the Sun God, where devotees fast and offer prayers to the setting sun. It's a significant festival in Jharkhand, showcasing the state's rich cultural heritage.
  • Holi, Diwali, and Dussehra: These festivals are celebrated with great enthusiasm in Jharkhand, showcasing the state's cultural diversity and traditions.
  • Jityaa Bhaiya Dooj: A festival dedicated to the love and affection between brothers and sisters, where brothers bless their sisters and promise to protect them.
  • Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha: Significant festivals in the Islamic calendar, celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm in Jharkhand.
