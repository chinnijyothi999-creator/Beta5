Transportation options:

By Air

  • Birsa Munda Airport, Ranchi: Located 150 km from Itkhori, this airport connects to major cities like Delhi, Hyderabad, and Mumbai. From the airport, hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Itkhori.
  • Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata: Located 423 km from Itkhori, this airport offers international flights. From the airport, take a flight or train to Ranchi or Hazaribagh, then proceed to Itkhori.

By Train

  • Hazaribagh Town Railway Station: Well-connected to major cities, this station is 50 km from Itkhori. Hire a taxi or take a bus from the station to reach Itkhori.
  • Chatra Railway Station: Although not well-connected, this station is 35 km from Itkhori. Limited public transport options are available.

By Road

  • Private Vehicles: Traveling by car or bike is convenient, with well-connected roads from Ranchi, Hazaribagh, and Chatra.
  • Buses and Taxis: Regular bus services operate from Ranchi, Hazaribagh, and Chatra to Itkhori. You can also hire taxis for a more comfortable journey.

Distances

  • Ranchi to Itkhori: 149-150 km
  • Hazaribagh to Itkhori: 50-70 km
  • Chatra to Itkhori: 35 km
  • Kolkata to Itkhori: 423-525 km

Cultural Activities and Events

Festivals and Events

  • Itkhori Mahotsav: A three-day cultural event celebrating the historical significance and religious diversity of Itkhori, where Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain traditions converge.
  • Janmashtami Celebration: A grand celebration at Itkhori Farmhouse with cultural events.
  • Kartik Purnima (Bishua): Devotees gather to bathe in the nearby reservoir and participate in religious rituals after Chhath Puja.
  • Basant Panchmi and Ram Navmi: Large numbers of pilgrims flock to the hill top for worship.

Cultural Significance

  • Bhadrakali Temple: A revered spiritual destination embodying a confluence of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, with intricate stone carvings and architectural styles characteristic of the Pala dynasty.
  • Buddhist Tradition: Itkhori holds significance in Buddhist traditions, believed to be the meditation spot of Mahatma Buddha.
  • Jain Heritage: The site is believed to be the birthplace of Bhagwan Sheetalnath, the 10th Tirthankara of Jainism.
